# Commercial Memory-System Funding Timeline

Covering the top-funded companies from the landscape catalog's T1/T2 commercial sections: dedicated memory layers, framework-embedded, platform-provider, coding-agent, browser-agent, personal AI/PKM, voice-first/wearable, and verticals.

---

## 2016–2022: Foundations

The earliest commercial memory-adjacent rounds predate the agent memory framing entirely. **Otter.ai** (then AISense) raised a $3M seed in September 2016 and a $10M Series A in November 2017, building transcription infrastructure that would later become the meeting-memory category. **Aleph Alpha** closed a €5.3M seed in late 2020 and a €23M Series A (Earlybird-led) in June 2021, establishing early European sovereign-AI ambitions that surface today in PhariaAI.

**Perplexity AI** began its run in September 2022 with a $3.1M seed from Elad Gil and Nat Friedman, with Andrej Karpathy and Yann LeCun among angels — an early signal that top AI researchers saw search-grounded memory as a distinct product category.

---

## 2023: Category Emergence

2023 was the year the agent-memory category first attracted institutional capital, though rounds were small.

**LangChain** (April 2023) raised a $10M seed from Benchmark, funding what was then primarily an open-source project. The embedded LangMem module came later, but this is when the framework-memory story began. By February 2024, Sequoia led a $25M Series A at a $200M valuation.

**AI21 Labs** closed a $155M Series C in August 2023 with Google and Nvidia participating, funding the Jamba SSM-Transformer hybrid that would reach 256K context and position AI21 as the architecture bet on long-context-as-memory.

**Aleph Alpha** raised its largest round — a €500M Series B in November 2023 co-led by Robert Bosch, Schwarz Group, and SAP — cementing European enterprise positioning and funding the PhariaCatch governed-document memory system.

**Replit** raised a $97.4M Series C (a16z-led) in 2023, making it the best-funded coding platform with implicit filesystem-as-memory architecture.

**Otter.ai** had already closed a $50M Series B in February 2021 (Spectrum Equity), establishing itself as the dominant meeting-memory brand well before the current wave.

**FlowiseAI** was accepted into Y Combinator (2023, $500K), keeping it lightly funded before Workday acquired it in August 2025.

---

## 2024: Dedicated Memory Layers Take Off — Q1–Q3

The first half of 2024 saw the dedicated memory layer category receive its first serious venture capital. The catalyst was Cognition's Devin launch and the ensuing AI agent gold rush.

**Zep** (YC W24) raised its first institutional capital in March 2024 — a $500K pre-seed from Y Combinator — then grew to $2.3M total with Engineering Capital and Step Function (Boston). Small absolute amount, but the YC stamp gave Graphiti's bi-temporal knowledge graph credibility at the right moment.

**Letta (MemGPT)** emerged from stealth on September 23, 2024 with a $10M seed led by Felicis Ventures at a $70M valuation. This was the clearest signal yet that dedicated memory was a standalone venture category: a Berkeley spinout built around OS-style memory tiering raised on research credibility alone. Jeff Dean, Clem Delangue, and Cristobal Valenzuela were among angels.

**Mem0** raised a $3.9M seed (Kindred Ventures-led) early in 2024, quietly positioning as the universal memory API before its larger raise.

**Vapi** raised a $2.1M seed from Kleiner Perkins and Abstract Ventures for voice-agent infrastructure that externalizes state into structured call variables rather than model recall.

**Vectorize** (which ships Hindsight, the biomimetic four-network memory service) raised a $3.6M seed in October 2024 from True Ventures.

**Retell AI** raised $4.6M seed (Alt Capital + YC) in mid-2024 for voice-agent long-term memory via CRM webhooks.

**CrewAI** closed $18M including a Series A led by Insight Partners in October 2024, Andrew Ng and Dharmesh Shah among angels — the largest single raise for any framework-embedded memory player to that point.

---

## 2024: Coding-Agent Memory Reaches Scale — Q2

**Augment Code** announced a $227M Series B at a $977M valuation in April 2024 (Sutter Hill, Index, Lightspeed, Meritech). Its Context Engine semantic knowledge graph and separate Agent Memory tier made it the best-funded dedicated coding-memory product, dwarfing all pure-play dedicated memory layers at the time.

**Devin (Cognition)** compressed two rounds into a month in March–April 2024: a $21M Series A and then a $175M follow-on, both led by Founders Fund, at a $2B valuation. The server-side Knowledge architecture with repo-pinned, global, and org-scoped tiers was the most aggressive memory model in the coding-agent space at the time.

---

## 2024: Browser and Platform Memory

**Browserbase** raised a seed in June 2024 and a Series A (Kleiner Perkins + CRV) in September 2024, building cloud browser infrastructure with persistent session state — the dev-infra layer under agentic browser memory.

**The Browser Company** (Dia, later acquired by Atlassian for $610M in September 2025) had raised approximately $128M in prior rounds before the acquisition.

**Granola** raised a $20M Series A (Spark Capital-led) in October 2024 after a $4.25M seed in May 2023, establishing the premium AI meeting-notepad category.

---

## Q4 2024: Voice AI

**Vapi** raised a $20M Series A led by Bessemer in December 2024 at ~$130M valuation — the first institutional round for a voice-agent memory infrastructure company, validating structured call-state as a memory approach.

---

## 2025: A Memory Layer Wave Breaks — H1

The first half of 2025 saw the largest absolute dollar amounts yet reach memory-adjacent products, though mostly in coding and consumer AI.

**Devin (Cognition)** raised $500M at a $9.8B valuation in August 2025, followed by $400M at $10.2B in September — a remarkable compression that briefly made it the most-valued AI memory product. Total raised exceeded $696M.

**Lovable** raised a $200M Series A (Accel-led) in early 2025 at a $1.8B valuation, validating filesystem-as-memory in the consumer coding category. Its $330M Series B (CapitalG + Menlo, December 2025) at $6.6B made it the most-valued app in this catalog.

**Granola** raised a $43M Series B (NFDG-led) in May 2025 at a $250M valuation.

**Replit** raised $250M (Prysm Capital-led) in September 2025 at a $3B valuation, with Google's AI Futures Fund participating.

**Perplexity AI** continued its rapid fundraising: $500M Series D (December 2024, $9B), $500M Series E (May 2025, $14B), $200M Series F (September 2025, $20B). With $1.72B total raised and Comet launching as a free AI-native browser in March 2026, it is the most-funded browser-memory company in this catalog.

## Q4 2025: The Dedicated Layer Gets Its First Big Round

**Mem0** announced a $24M Series A in October 2025 — $3.9M seed (Kindred) plus a $20M new tranche led by Basis Set Ventures at a $150M valuation, with Peak XV, GitHub Fund, and Y Combinator participating. AWS Agent SDK exclusivity made this the clearest proof of dedicated memory layer commercial traction to date.

**Supermemory** raised $2.6M–$3M seed (Susa Ventures) in October 2025, with Jeff Dean and Logan Kilpatrick among angels.

**LangChain** raised a $125M Series B (IVP-led) at a $1.25B unicorn valuation in October 2025.

**AI21 Labs** raised a $300M Series D (NVIDIA + Alphabet-led) in May 2025.

**Sandbar** raised $13M from True Ventures / Upfront / Betaworks in November 2025 to launch the Stream AI smart ring.

## 2026: Seed Wave for Newer Dedicated Layers — Q1–Q2

Early 2026 brought a cluster of fresh seed rounds specifically for dedicated-memory products, marking the point when the category attracted early-stage capital broadly rather than just one or two names.

**Cognee** closed a €7.5M (~$8.15M) seed led by Pebblebed (Pamela Vagata, Keith Adams) in February 2026, with 42CAP and Vermilion Ventures. The ECL pipeline targeting Claude Agent SDK and Google ADK integrations.

**Trace** raised $3M seed in February 2026 (YC + Zeno + Transpose + Goodwater) for a context-graph approach to institutional knowledge.

**Nyne** raised $5.3M seed (Wischoff Ventures + South Park Commons) in March 2026 for people-graph memory.

**Interloom** raised $16.5M seed (DN Capital-led) in March 2026 — the largest early-stage dedicated-memory raise in Q1, targeting enterprise tacit-knowledge capture with customers including Zurich Insurance and Volkswagen.

**Granola** raised $125M Series C (Index + Kleiner) in March 2026 at a $1.5B valuation, expanding from meeting notes to enterprise AI context — total raised reached $192M.

**Sandbar** raised $23M Series A (Adjacent + Kindred) in March 2026 for the Stream ring.

**MemoraX AI** raised ~$10M seed (L2F Light Source) in March 2026 (Shenzhen) for in-model endogenous memory trained via agentic RL.

**Replit** raised $400M Series D (Georgian-led) in March 2026 at a $9B valuation.

**NeoCognition** raised a $40M seed (Cambium Capital + Walden Catalyst) in April 2026 for experiential world-model agents; Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan and Databricks' Ion Stoica among angels.

**Era Computer** raised $11M total ($2M pre-seed from Topology/Betaworks, $9M seed from Abstract Ventures/BoxGroup) in April 2026 for a pluggable memory OS for AI wearables.

## Key Observations

**Wave 1 (2023):** Framework and platform plays raised first — LangChain, AI21, Aleph Alpha. Dedicated memory layers had not yet emerged as a standalone category.

**Wave 2 (H1 2024):** Coding-agent memory received the largest checks: Augment Code ($252M total) and Cognition ($175M+), both dwarfing pure-play memory layers. Dedicated layer startups raised $2–10M.

**Wave 3 (Q4 2024–Q1 2025):** Coding and consumer AI reached scale (Devin, Lovable, Replit). Dedicated layers remained in single/low-double-digit millions.

**Wave 4 (Q4 2025):** Mem0's Series A and LangChain's unicorn round confirmed the memory-infrastructure investment thesis. $150M Mem0 valuation is modest vs coding peers but structurally important.

**Wave 5 (Q1 2026):** A distinct seed cluster for purpose-built dedicated-memory companies (Cognee, Interloom, Nyne, Trace, NeoCognition) — the first time multiple dedicated-layer companies raised in the same quarter. Indicates the category has separated from "agent framework with memory" into a distinct investment vertical.

The valuation gap between the best-funded coding-agent products (Cognition at $10B, Lovable at $6.6B, Replit at $9B) and the best-funded dedicated memory layer (Mem0 at $150M) remains the defining structural feature of the landscape as of May 2026.
