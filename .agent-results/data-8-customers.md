# Customer, ARR, and Employee Data — AI Memory Systems Landscape

**Scope:** T1 + T2 commercial rows from landscape.html sections: Dedicated memory layers, Framework-embedded memory, Platform-provider memory, Coding-agent memory, Browser-agent memory, Personal AI / PKM, Voice-first / wearable, Verticals.

**Coverage:** 52 systems researched. Focus on top ~30 by funding/visibility. Data as of May 2026.

---

## Systems with the Strongest Enterprise Traction (Named Logos)

### Tier 1: Rich Named-Customer Evidence

**Glean** is the clearest enterprise winner in this dataset. Their December 2025 $200M ARR announcement named Booking.com, Comcast, eBay, Intuit, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Samsung, and Zillow, and subsequent press added TIME, Dell, T-Mobile, Databricks, Duolingo, Grammarly, Plaid, Uber, Reddit, Confluent, and Motive. That is 18+ named logos — more than any other memory-adjacent system in the table. They are the only pure-play AI workplace search / memory company with a public customer page and detailed case studies. 1,000+ employees. US-headquartered (Palo Alto).

**Devin (Cognition)** has published named enterprise logos: Goldman Sachs, Citi, Dell, Cisco, Ramp, Palantir, Nubank, and Mercado Libre. Their coding-agent ARR grew from $1M to $73M in nine months (Sep 2024–Jun 2025), with the Windsurf acquisition pushing combined run-rate to ~$150M by mid-2025. These are the strongest named customer disclosures among coding-agent memory systems.

**Interloom** is a German outlier: despite being a small early-stage startup (~19 employees, $16.5M seed), they published five named customers — Zurich Insurance, JLL, Fiege, Commerzbank, and Volkswagen — in their March 2026 funding announcement. The Commerzbank case study (50% → 5% knowledge gap) and Zurich Insurance (won company-wide AI competition) are unusually specific.

**Aleph Alpha PhariaAI** has named public-sector and enterprise customers: German federal ministries, Bavarian state administration, the Bundeswehr, Siemens, and BMW. Revenue was minimal prior to Cohere merger discussions (April 2026), but sovereign AI positioning in EU defense and DAX corporations gives them a distinct enterprise footprint.

**AI21 Labs (Jamba)** published customer names: Boston Consulting Group, Bain & Company, Moderna, Amgen, Gates Foundation, JetBlue, Booking.com. Revenue of $57.8M in 2024 with ~240 employees. These are management consulting and life-science customers, consistent with their enterprise-focused model positioning.

**Granola** named Vanta, Gusto, Thumbtack, Asana, Cursor, Lovable, Decagon, and Mistral AI as customers. Most are mid-size tech companies rather than large enterprises, but the list is unusually specific for a meeting-notes product. Their March 2026 $125M Series C at $1.5B valuation shows strong momentum.

### Tier 2: Aggregate Metrics Without Named Logos

**Notion AI** ($600M ARR, 75% of Fortune 500) and **Replit** ($253M ARR, 85% Fortune 500) both cite Fortune 500 penetration but disclose only a small set of named accounts (Kaiser Permanente, Nvidia, Volvo, Coinbase, Mercedes-Benz, Zillow). The ARR numbers are real and public; the customer list specificity is modest.

**Perplexity** ($200M ARR as of early 2026, reaching $450M+ annualized by March 2026) discloses 20,000+ enterprise organizations and names Snapchat as a platform partner, but does not publish individual enterprise logos on the memory feature specifically.

**Otter.ai** reached $100M ARR in March 2025 and claims 35M users. No named enterprise logos on the memory product.

**Anthropic Claude Memory** names Rakuten as an early-adopter with specific metrics (97% fewer first-pass errors, 27% lower cost, 34% lower latency). That is a single but unusually precise enterprise case study.

## Employee Count Distribution

The dataset splits into three clusters:

**Micro teams (1–50 employees):** Mem0 (~4–8), Supermemory (~8), Letta (~13), Omi (~small), Limitless (pre-acquisition ~small), Bee (pre-acquisition ~small). These are the pure-play memory layer startups where the product is memory infrastructure, not an application. High GitHub star counts but not yet at commercial scale.

**Growth stage (51–500 employees):** Granola (~80–100, growing 49% in 6 months), Cognition/Devin, Augment Code (~172–188), Browserbase (~43–55), Vapi (~153), Lovable (~146), Tabnine (~71), LangChain (~163). This cluster contains the strongest commercial traction relative to size: Lovable at $400M ARR with 146 employees is the most striking revenue-per-employee number in the table.

**Large (1000+):** Glean (1,000–1,500+), Perplexity (~1,200–1,500+), Replit (~230–400), and all major platform providers (OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Meta, LinkedIn).

- **Canada:** Voiceflow (Toronto), Botpress (Montreal)

European commercial traction is led by Interloom (German enterprise customers) and Aleph Alpha (German sovereign/defense).

## What Most Systems Don't Disclose

Of 52 systems researched: approximately 30 (58%) have no publicly disclosed named enterprise customers. Most standalone memory layer startups — Mem0, Zep, Graphiti, Cognee, Supermemory, Memvid, Memobase, ByteRover, OMEGA, Backboard — publish no customer logos. For framework-embedded systems (LangMem, CrewAI, Mastra, smolagents) the "customer" is the framework adopter, not a named enterprise.

ARR is null for the majority. Only 14 of 52 systems have any public revenue data. Voice-first and wearable products (Omi, Plaud, Bee, Sandbar, Granola) publish user counts or hardware sales but rarely ARR.

## Key Insights

1. **Glean is the definitive enterprise traction leader** for memory-adjacent systems by named-logo count (18+) and ARR ($200M). No other company in the dedicated-memory-layer tier is within an order of magnitude on either metric.

2. **The coding-agent segment (Devin, Augment, Lovable, Replit)** collectively shows the strongest revenue velocity, but the "memory" in those products is incidental to the coding workflow rather than a standalone memory product.

3. **Pure-play memory layer startups** (Mem0, Zep, Letta, Cognee, Supermemory) are pre-revenue-scale despite high developer adoption. The largest is Letta at ~$1.4M. None have disclosed named enterprise customers at meaningful scale.

4. **Meeting-notes products** (Otter.ai, Granola, Read.ai) are the most mature voice/audio memory category by ARR. Otter.ai at $100M ARR with fewer than 200 employees is the revenue efficiency standout.

5. **Wearable/hardware memory** remains pre-enterprise: Plaud (1M consumer users, no enterprise logos), Omi (open-source consumer), Bee and Limitless both acquired before reaching enterprise scale.

6. **German enterprise** is the most visible non-US enterprise cluster: Interloom has 5 named German enterprise customers despite being an 18-month-old startup.
