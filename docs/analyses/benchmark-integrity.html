Benchmark integrity

The companion to benchmark coverage. That view answers who benchmarks on what. This view answers the next question: are the numbers trustworthy? Every benchmark mention in the catalog is classified by where the citation lives — peer-reviewed venue, neutral third party, the vendor’s own site — and cross-checked against contradicting claims from other catalog rows. The canonical disputed case (Mem0 vs Zep on LoCoMo) shows up directly in the LoCoMo row of the per-benchmark breakdown.

169 classified benchmark mentions across the 894-record catalog · 25 distinct benchmarks tracked.

Classification rules

Peer-reviewed
Citation host is a peer-reviewed venue (arXiv, OpenReview, ACL Anthology, IEEE Xplore, ACM DL, PMLR).
Independently verified
Citation host is a non-vendor third party — neutral leaderboard, independent blog, or commodity publishing platform.
Vendor-claimed
Citation host matches the vendor’s own domain. The vendor is making the claim about its own product.
Disputed
Another catalog row contradicts this number — either via in-cell dispute signal (⚠, "rebuttal") or score divergence > 7 absolute points on the same benchmark.
Unverifiable
No citation that resolves to a host, sentinel value, or depth-floor-reached cell.

A row can carry one or more gaming flags independent of its bucket — those surface specific gaming patterns (vendor self-defined, weak baseline, single sub-task). See the panel below.
