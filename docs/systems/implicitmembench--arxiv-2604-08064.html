ImplicitMemBench

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08064

300 items across three paradigms: procedural memory (one-shot skill retention after interference), priming (theme-driven response biases), classical conditioning (CS-US exposure shaping first decisions). Unified learning-interference-test protocol with first-attempt scoring to isolate automatized behavior — distinct from explicit-recall benchmarks.

Pros & cons

Pros

First benchmark targeting implicit (non-declarative) memory in LLMs; exposes a category of memory failure invisible to existing explicit-memory benchmarks.

Cons

300 items is small; "classical conditioning" in LLMs is contested mechanistically — operationalization may reflect prompt-following rather than true conditioning.

Claims & capabilities

17 models evaluated; no model exceeds 66% overall. Top: DeepSeek-R1 65.3%, Qwen3-32B 64.1%, GPT-5 63.0%. Reveals dramatic asymmetries (inhibition 17.6% vs preference 75.0%).
